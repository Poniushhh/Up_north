The Classic Bloody Mary

The Bloody Mary is one of the most iconic and beloved cocktails in the world, famous for its bold savory flavor and reputation as the ultimate brunch companion. This vodka-based drink combines tomato juice with a complex blend of spices, creating a unique cocktail experience that's both refreshing and satisfying.

Origin: The cocktail's exact origin is debated, but the most popular story credits Fernand Petiot, a bartender at Harry's New York Bar in Paris during the 1920s. He later refined the recipe at New York's St. Regis Hotel in the 1930s, where it evolved from a simple vodka and tomato juice mixture into the spicy, complex drink we know today.

History: Originally called the "Red Snapper" to avoid the controversial name, the Bloody Mary gained popularity in the 1950s as a hangover cure and morning cocktail. Its savory profile and customizable nature made it a staple of American brunch culture.

Why It's Special: Unlike most cocktails, the Bloody Mary is endlessly customizable. From mild to extremely spicy, from simple to garnished with entire meals, each bartender and venue puts their own spin on this classic.
